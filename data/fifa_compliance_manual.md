# FIFA World Cup 2026 Venue Operations Compliance Manual

This compliance manual defines standard operating procedures (SOPs) for SoFi Stadium during FIFA World Cup 2026 matches.

---

## 1. Crowd Management and Gate SOPs
- **Capacity Thresholds**: Gates are designed for a maximum throughput of 4,000 fans per hour.
- **Congestion Classification**:
  - **Low**: Count < 1,500/hr. Wait time < 5 min.
  - **Medium**: Count 1,500–2,500/hr. Wait time 5–10 min.
  - **High**: Count 2,500–3,500/hr. Wait time 10–20 min. Alternate gates recommended.
  - **Critical**: Count > 3,500/hr. Wait time > 20 min. **IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED**. Reroute fans to the nearest low-congestion gate. Hold inbound transit streams if necessary.
- **Safety Hold**: If any gate congestion becomes "Critical", command staff must be alerted immediately and safety volunteers dispatched to guide crowd splits.

---

## 2. Waste and Sustainability SOPs
- **Waste Bin Monitoring**: Overflowing bins present safety and environmental compliance hazards. Bins must be emptied within 15 minutes of an alert.
- **Issue Classification**:
  - Classification is automated by volunteer report intake.
  - Bins near food concessions must be checked hourly.
  - Recycle bins are color-coded (blue) and must be sorted at dispatch stations.

---

## 3. Accessibility SOPs
- **Ramp Compliance**: Ramps must be kept clear of congestion at all times.
- **Assistance Dispatch**: If a fan requests accessibility assistance at a congested gate (e.g., Gate A1 has no ramp), operations must dispatch a mobility assistant to guide them to Gate A2 or Gate B1 which are equipped with compliant ramps.
